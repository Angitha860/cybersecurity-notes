# Phishing

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware.

## Goals of Phishing

- Steal usernames and passwords
- Steal banking information
- Deliver malware
- Gain unauthorized access

## Common Signs of Phishing

### Suspicious Sender

Example:

support@amaz0n-security.com

### Urgent Messages

Examples:

- Act now!
- Account suspended!
- Verify immediately!

### Suspicious Links

Real:

amazon.com

Fake:

amazon-security-login.com

### Unexpected Attachments

Examples:

- invoice.exe
- payment.zip

## Types of Phishing

### Email Phishing

Mass phishing emails sent to many users.

### Spear Phishing

Targeted phishing against a specific individual.

### Whaling

Phishing attacks targeting executives.

### Smishing

Phishing through SMS messages.

### Vishing

Phishing through voice calls.

## Prevention

- Verify sender addresses
- Inspect links carefully
- Avoid unexpected attachments
- Enable MFA
- Report suspicious emails

## What I Learned

- Phishing is a social engineering attack.
- Attackers use emails, SMS, and phone calls.
- MFA helps reduce phishing impact.
- SOC analysts frequently investigate phishing incidents.

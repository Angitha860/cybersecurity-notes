# Log Retention

## What is Log Retention?

Log retention is the practice of storing logs for a specific period of time so they can be used for monitoring, investigations, auditing, and compliance purposes.

## Why Log Retention is Important

Many security incidents are discovered days, weeks, or months after they occur.

Retained logs help analysts:

* Investigate historical events
* Build attack timelines
* Identify suspicious activity
* Support incident response

## Example

Attack Occurs
↓
30 Days Pass
↓
Suspicious Activity Discovered

If logs were retained, analysts can investigate the attack.

If logs were deleted, important evidence may be lost.

## Common Retention Periods

* 7 Days
* 30 Days
* 90 Days
* 1 Year or More

The retention period depends on business requirements, compliance requirements, and storage capacity.

## Key Takeaway

Log retention helps security teams investigate past incidents by preserving important evidence.
